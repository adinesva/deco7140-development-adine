The website is made to provide the impression that it is a friendly, reliable, and empowering resource for foreign communities coming to Australia. Every visitor should get a sense that they have entered a place designed specifically for them, a secure, welcoming center where support is constantly available and diversity is welcomed.


  • Inclusive and Friendly: using approachable language, warm visuals, and multicultural representation so users feel they belong.
  • Credible and Reliable: ensuring all information is accurate, verified, and clearly presented to build confidence and trust.
  • Empowering and Supportive: encouraging users to not only access resources, but also share their own stories, join communities, and contribute to shaping their new life.

Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples


Using culturally appropriate language when addressing Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples.

Example: Using the word 'Indigenous Australians'.


Adressing Age diversity


When addressing age, use respectful language and using consistent style. Avoiding addressing age when it's not relevant.

Example: Avoid using words like 'old' and 'young', and use numerals while describing age.


Adressing Culutral and Language diversity


Using inclusive and respectful language while addressing cultures, as Australia is diverse and have many culture.

Example: Using the term "multicultural communities". Refer to people living in Australia as "Australians". Use the words "migrants, immigrants and new arrivals" to address people recently arrived in Australia.


Adressing Gender and Sexual diversity
