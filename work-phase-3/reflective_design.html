The website is made to provide the impression that it is a friendly, reliable, and empowering resource for foreign communities coming to Australia. Every visitor should get a sense that they have entered a place designed specifically for them, a secure, welcoming center where support is constantly available and diversity is welcomed.


  • Inclusive and Friendly: using approachable language, warm visuals, and multicultural representation so users feel they belong.
  • Credible and Reliable: ensuring all information is accurate, verified, and clearly presented to build confidence and trust.
  • Empowering and Supportive: encouraging users to not only access resources, but also share their own stories, join communities, and contribute to shaping their new life.

Accessibility Report

Findings from the IBM Equal Access Accessibility Checker


Issue 1

Use the fieldset element to group logically related input elements


Relevant WCAG Success Criteria:

1.3.1: Info and Relationship
3.3.2: Labels or Instructions


Element Affected

Upload photo in forms, in Behavioural Design page


This will affect users with screen readers, the screen readers won't know that the file input is part of a distinct group of fields. This will make the form harder to understand, and will cause misinterpretation.


Use the fieldset and legend elements to group logically related form fields together.

Issue 2

Text contrast of 2.88 with its background is less than the WCAG AA minimum requirements for text of size 13.3333px and weight of 400


Relevant WCAG Success Criteria:

1.4.3: Contrast (Minimum)
1.4.6: Contrast (Enhanced)


Element Affected

Text in Paragraph


This will affect users with low vision, due to lack of contrast between text and background. Also, users with color vision deficiencies, may not be able to differentiate between text and background.


Increase the contrast ratio of the text and background to meet at least 4.5:1 for normal text.
Currently, I use the the hex color #999999 for the text, next I will use #333333 to make the contrast stronger.

Issue 3

Table has no headers identified


Relevant WCAG Success Criteria:

1.3.1: Info and Relationship
1.3.2 Meaningful Sequence


Element Affected

Table, in Behavioural Design page


This will affect users with screen readers, they will hear long list of data without context. This makes users difficult and unable to compare data. Accessibility is compromised because information that relies on table structure is lost or confusing.


Use the tag th instead of th, referring to semantic HTML guidelines. Add the scope attribute (scope="col" for column headers, scope="row" for row headers)
