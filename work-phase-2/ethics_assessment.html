Ethics Assessment

Ethics guidelines based on Australian Computing Society's (ACS) Code of Professional Ethics.

Identifying the Potential Harms

What harm might occur to people from the subscription plug-in

  • Privacy Breaches: Subscribers may experience spam, phishing, or even fraud if emails are compromised or exposed (particularly international community s who might already feel insecure in a new place).
  • Loss of Trust: People may completely distrust this platform if they believe they were duped into providing more information than was necessary.
  • Unwanted surveillance: Without transparency, data collection may give users the impression that they are being monitored.

What data would you normally expect to share when signing up for a newsletter?

  • Email Address
  • First and Last Name
  • Consent checkbox

What is being collected that is unexpected?

  • Phone Number
  • Location Data
  • Demographic Details (age, gender, occupation, etc)
  • Behavioral tracking (e.g., which links you click, device type, time of open).

Why is this data being collected?

  • For marketing segmentation
  • To resell data to advertisers and third-party
  • To track user behavior and improve engagement.

What are the possible consequences for users?

  • Users get profiled without consent.
  • Risk of data being misused or shared with third parties.
  • Users might avoid subscribing even if the resource could benefit them.

Navigating Tensions

What tensions might exist between you and your client?

  • Client wants more data vs. Ethical minimalism. Client may request to collect segmented and detailed data, but the solution is we should only collect necessary data.
  • Client wants frequent engagement vs. User well-being. Client may request for daily and aggresive emails. But it would be considered as spam.
  • Client wants speed/cost savings vs. Professionalism & security. The client may recommend a cheap third-party plug-in with inadequate privacy protections. Secured system is needed even though it can costs more.

What options could you present to navigate these tensions

  • Evidence-based persuasion. Present studies or case studies that demonstrate how privacy-first design increases engagement and trust over the long run.
  • Offer alternatives. For instance, provide choices for segmented frequency in instead of daily emails.
  • Transparency trade-offs. Make further data collection optional and clearly justified if it is required.
  • Risk framing. Describe the dangers of violating ACS values, such as damage to one's reputation and legal non-compliance.
