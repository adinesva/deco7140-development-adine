Opening a Bank Account in Australia

Setting up a local bank account is one of the first steps for international students. Most major banks offer student-friendly accounts with no monthly fees and easy online access — making it simple to manage rent, bills, and everyday expenses.

Transport

Getting Around Australia: Public Transport Made Simple

Australia’s major cities offer reliable public transport networks — including buses, trains, trams, and ferries — that make it easy to explore and commute affordably. Most cities use a reloadable travel card system, such as the Go Card in Queensland or Opal Card in New South Wales. Learn how to top up, plan your routes, and travel like a local from day one.

Affordable and Eco-Friendly Travel Options for Students

Cycling, carpooling, and student concession fares are great ways to save money and reduce your carbon footprint. Many campuses have dedicated bike lanes and end-of-trip facilities, while state transport apps help you track routes and timetables in real time. Explore how you can stay mobile, sustainable, and budget-smart while studying in Australia.

Understanding Work Rights for International Students

Before starting any job in Australia, it's important to know your work rights and visa conditions. Most student visas allow part-time work up to a set number of hours each fortnight. Learn about fair pay, workplace safety, and how to protect yourself from scams or unpaid work.

Finding Part-Time and Casual Jobs in Australia

Many international students and newcomers find their first jobs in retail, hospitality, or university support roles. Explore online job boards, career hubs, and local community postings to find flexible work that fits your schedule. With the right preparation and resume, landing your first job in Australia is easier than you think.
